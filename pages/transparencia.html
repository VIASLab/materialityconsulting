Where We Fall Short

True corporate transparency means admitting limitations. We do not claim perfect sustainability, but we do promise constant improvement.

As a consulting entity, our primary carbon footprint is indirect. We analyze where our supply chain faces hurdles and lay out concrete, transparent mitigation pathways.

Challenge 01

Scope 3 Supply Chain (Cloud Computing)

The Reality: Providing educational resource hubs and metrics calculation templates relies on cloud hosting providers. While we utilize carbon-neutral regions, server loads still draw physical power from local grids.

Mitigation Plan: We underwent a full codebase refactor, migrating this site from WordPress to clean, framework-free HTML/CSS/JS. We reduced our page weight by 95%, directly lowering data transfer and CPU usage for every visitor.

Challenge 02

Client Indirect Mitigation Control

The Reality: We audit and guide clients to establish financially material ESG targets. However, as independent consultants, we have no legal voting power on client board of directors to enforce final mitigation budget executions.

Mitigation Plan: We are expanding our educational resources to focus heavily on the fiduciary duties of board members, demonstrating that ignoring material ESG factors violates legal duties of care to shareholders.
